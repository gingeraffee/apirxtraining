POL-06 — Confidentiality & Data Privacy (Employee Information, Company Data, and Electronic Systems)
System
BambooHR (employee records), HR SharePoint/Network Drives (documents), Email/Computers (electronic communication)
Owner
HR Department
When
At all times; whenever handling employee information, business records, or company systems
Output
Confidential information protected; records accessed only by authorized users; compliant storage, sharing, and retention
Before you start
All employees sign a confidentiality and non-disclosure agreement upon hire; refusal is grounds for immediate termination.
Personnel files are company property and access to information is restricted based on state and federal regulations.
Employees may review their own personnel files only through HR, with reasonable notice, in AAP’s offices and in the presence of an appointed representative.
Records related to health prepared by or under the supervision of a health care provider are subject to HIPAA standards.
Computers, files, email, and software are company property intended for business use. Employees must not access files or stored communications without authorization; usage may be monitored.
Certain complaints (e.g., harassment) will be treated as confidential to the extent possible, while still allowing the company to investigate and respond appropriately.
Steps
Step
Action
1
Identify the information type before sharing or storing: employee records (personnel/payroll/benefits), medical information, candidate/background data, business records, or proprietary/confidential company information.
2
Apply “need-to-know” access: share confidential information only with individuals who require it to perform their job duties. If unsure, pause and consult HR.
3
Personnel file requests: If an employee asks to view their personnel file, route the request to HR. HR will schedule the review with reasonable advance notice, in AAP’s offices, with an appointed representative present.
4
Medical and leave-related information: Treat medical information as highly confidential. Do not request or distribute medical details. Route medical documentation and sensitive leave questions to HR.
5
Electronic systems use: Use company computers/email for business purposes. Do not use passwords or access files or stored communications without authorization. Assume electronic usage may be monitored.
6
Email and sharing controls: Verify recipient(s) before sending. Use only approved storage locations (BambooHR folders / HR SharePoint / designated drives). Do not forward confidential documents to personal email or store them on personal devices.
7
Printing and physical handling: Print confidential documents only when necessary. Keep documents secured when not in use. Do not leave files unattended in public/shared spaces. Collect documents immediately from printers/copiers. Shred sensitive documents as soon as possible.
8
Retention and disposal: Store records in the correct system/folder per HR practices. When disposal is approved, use secure shredding for paper and approved deletion methods for electronic files.
9
Complaints and investigations: Treat complaints as confidential to the extent possible. Do not discuss details with coworkers/supervisors unless directed by HR as part of an investigation or required business need.
10
Report concerns: Immediately notify HR if you suspect a privacy breach (misdirected email, lost documents, unauthorized access, inappropriate disclosure, or suspicious system activity).
Checks
Confidential information shared only on a need-to-know basis; personnel file access routed through HR; medical information handled under HIPAA standards; electronic systems accessed only with authorization; sensitive documents stored in approved HR systems; physical documents secured; privacy incidents reported promptly.
Escalate to HR Manager immediately if
You suspect a data breach or unauthorized disclosure; an employee requests personnel file access; medical/leave documentation is involved; a complaint involves harassment/discrimination/retaliation; legal/compliance questions arise; or you are unsure whether information can be shared.
