CA-01 — Corrective Action Process (Documentation, Delivery, and Filing)
System
BambooHR (Employee Files) and HR SharePoint (templates/archive)
Owner
Managers (initiate) + HR (guidance) + HR Administrative Assistant (scanning/uploading)
When
When performance or conduct concerns require formal documentation or progressive discipline
Output
Completed corrective action documentation delivered to employee; filed in BambooHR Corrective Action folder
Before you start
Corrective action may be issued for insubordination, carelessness, unsatisfactory productivity, poor attitude, violations of the employee handbook/code of conduct, loud/boisterous/inflammatory statements to coworkers, and other performance or conduct issues.
Use the Employee Warning Template (available in BambooHR or the HR SharePoint).
Managers are responsible for issuing corrective action and providing completed documents to HR for recordkeeping.
HR provides guidance on approach (goals, LinkedIn Learning, PIP, etc.) as needed; each situation is handled case-by-case.
Related process note (Attendance Point Warnings)
Point Warnings are issued by HR and delivered by the employee’s direct supervisor.
Point Coaching occurs at 5 points; verbal warning at 6 points; written warning at 7 points; termination at 8 points (termination is at supervisor discretion).
Steps
Step
Action
1
Manager identifies the issue (performance or conduct) and documents facts: dates/times, specific behaviors, policy expectations, and impact.
2
Manager consults HR as needed for guidance on next steps (coaching goals, LinkedIn Learning course assignment, Performance Improvement Plan (PIP), or formal corrective action).
3
If formal corrective action is appropriate, manager completes the Employee Warning Template (BambooHR or HR SharePoint). Include: reason, expectations, corrective steps, follow-up timeline, and consequences.
4
Manager meets with the employee to deliver the corrective action. Employee acknowledgment/signature is obtained when applicable (signature acknowledges receipt, not necessarily agreement).
5
Manager provides the completed corrective action documentation to HR (including any supporting documents referenced).
6
HR Administrative Assistant scans the documentation (if paper) and uploads it to the employee’s Corrective Action folder in BambooHR. Verify the file is readable and labeled consistently (date + type + reason).
7
If the employee receives three (3) corrective actions for the same reason, HR and leadership review for next steps, which may include termination (per policy and circumstances).
Checks
Correct template used; reason stated clearly; expectations and timeline documented; consequences stated; employee received the document; manager provided completed paperwork to HR; HR Administrative Assistant scanned and uploaded to BambooHR Corrective Action folder; file name/date are correct and document is readable; repeat issues tracked (3 for same reason triggers review).
Escalate to HR Manager if
Situation involves harassment, discrimination, retaliation, threats/violence, safety risk, suspected policy violations requiring investigation, medical/disability accommodation concerns, employee requests leave related to a medical condition, or termination is being considered; or when three corrective actions for the same reason have occurred and next-step review is needed.
