FMLA-01 — Family and Medical Leave Act (FMLA) – Request Intake and Confidential Handling
System
Internal Certification Forms
Owner
HR Manager
When
Immediately after the employee asks about FMLA
Output
Confidential FMLA Case File; certification issues/received; leave designation communication to employee only
Key Points:
Escalate immediately! If an employee asks about FMLA (or even hints at a need for medical leave,) notify the HR Manager right away.
Confidentiality: FMLA information is confidential and cannot be shared with anyone, including supervisors
Do not promise approval: Eligibility and designation must be confirmed by the HR Manager.
AAP does not pay for FMLA time and employees are not required to exhaust all of their PTO before using unpaid FMLA time.
FMLA may be taken in 1 hour increments.
Eligibility & Entitlement
Eligibility: Employee must have worked at least 366 days and 1,250 hours in the last 12 months.
Entitlement: Employees receive 480 hours to use for a full 12-month period.
Pay Status: FMLA is unpaid.
PTO Requirements: Employees are not required to use their PTO before taking FMLA.
Steps
Step
Action
1
Employee asks or hints about needing FMLA leave for themselves or a family member.
2
Immediately escalate to HR Manager. Do not discuss details or request medical information. You are able to provide general answers, such as the eligibility requirements and pay status.
3
HR Manager provides the employee with the required FMLA Certification and explains submission requirements and deadlines.
4
Employee completes the certification and the HR Manager processes the time in BambooHR.
5
If the employee misses more than 30 consecutive work days, they may be required to provide a doctor’s note, a new certification, or fit-for-duty paperwork from their doctor.
Checks:
FMLA inquiries escalated immediately to HR Manager; confidentiality maintained; eligibility verified (366 days + 1,250 hours); certification issued and returned; total usage tracked against 480-hour annual entitlement; 30+ consecutive work-day absences include status/RTW updates to HR Manager.
Escalate to HR Manager if:
Any employee asks about FMLA; any employee references a serious health condition, ongoing treatment, or need for extended leave; questions arise about eligibility, certification, leave designation, or confidentiality; or an absence approaches/exceeds 30 consecutive work days.
